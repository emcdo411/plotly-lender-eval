## 5. DIL — Why ServiceLink Scores Lower on This Specific Scan

Three structural reasons, not a capability judgment:

1. **Ownership structure suppresses public signal.** ServiceLink is a majority-owned subsidiary of Fidelity National Financial, not a standalone public reporting entity the way First American (NYSE: FAF) is. FAF has securities-disclosure and investor-relations incentives to publicize AI wins quarterly. ServiceLink has no equivalent public-market pressure to announce internal tooling.
2. **Business model differs from Cotality's.** Cotality sells data infrastructure *as the product* — AI-platform visibility (Araya, Databricks, Snowflake) is core to its go-to-market. ServiceLink sells settlement/title *services*; the technology (EXOS) is the delivery mechanism, not the product being marketed to Wall Street or the press.
3. **EXOS itself is a real, verified asset** — the composite score understates this. EXOS is `[PUBLIC-VERIFIED]` as an award-recognized, proprietary, end-to-end platform. What's genuinely missing from the public record is a *named, recent, AI-specific* capability announcement at the scale of AgentNet Assist or Araya — that gap is real, but it's a communications/positioning gap, not necessarily a technology gap.

**DIL Gap Block:** Whether ServiceLink has unpublicized internal AI/BI tooling comparable to competitors cannot be determined from public sources. This is a **Knowable Unknown** — answerable once you have internal visibility in your first 30–60 days, not before.
